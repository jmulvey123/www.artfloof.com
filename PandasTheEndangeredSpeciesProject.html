Pandas are truly unique creatures. Pandas are important to the ecosystem because they balance it out. When learning about Pandas most people think about the black and white fur and the gentle animals. What many people don’t know is how these animals are valued and protected.

🪞Appearance🐼

Have you ever wondered why pandas have black and white fur? It's because the black fur absorbs heat so that they are warm in the winter and the white fur makes heat bounce off of it so it is cool in the summer. The fur is thick to help keep them warm in the cold mountains.

They have broad molar teeth to chew bamboo and an enlarged wrist bone to help them eat bamboo.

Pandas are also known to look absolutely adorable. Especially when they are a few weeks old, then they are the cutest! Smithsonian magazine has this to say about their cute features.

 “Neuroscientists speculate that their snub noses, generous cheeks and toddling gaits may excite circuitry in our brains normally related to interactions with human infants. Likewise, babies have supersized eyes, and pandas’ trademark black patches make their eyes appear larger by a factor of ten.”

🧸Offspring🍼

Pandas mate in the spring season from March-May. When the baby is born, the male panda leaves the mother. Pandas usually have two children at a time, but unfortunately, it is often that one baby panda dies.

Newborn pandas are very pink and do not have a lot of fur. They can’t see or hear. When newborn pandas are born, they weigh 3-5 ounces. That means that newborn pandas are 1/900 of their mother’s size! That makes newborn pandas the smallest mammal compared to their mothers!

Panda bears are VERY good mothers. When they have children, they hold their cubs almost constantly! They never leave the cub alone, even to get water for about a week. Then the mother carries the baby (in their mouths, like cats do with kittens) and hides the cub in a patch of bamboo while she gets food.

 The baby stays with the mother for about two years. After that the baby is no longer a baby. It is now a full grown panda, so it leaves the mother to find a mate. In mating season, male pandas compete with each other for a female panda to mate with them.

🧋Diet 🍜

Pandas eat bamboo 99% of the time, but they eat small animals, eggs and other things too. That means that they are omnivores. Did you know that bamboo is not very nutritious? Since pandas eat bamboo mostly, they have to spend 12 hours eating to get good nutrients. They also have a larger wrist bone to help eat bamboo more efficiently.   Pandas do not have predators, but younger baby pandas are prey to some animals including jackals, snow leopards (also endangered) and yellow-throated martens. Luckily, pandas are usually very good mothers and can scare their child's predators away.  

🇨🇳Habitat🏝️

As you should know, Pandas live in asia. But which part of asia? Pandas live in the mountains of south central China and in Shaanxi, Sichuan and Gansu provinces.

  Pandas don’t hibernate in the winter, unlike other bears. They migrate to the lower slopes and valleys where the air is warmer. Pandas also have a wide range of land.

They mark their territory so that other pandas keep away from their food supply. Smithsonian’s National Zoo and Conservation Biology Institute says that “giant pandas used to live in lowland areas, but farming, forest clearing and other development now restrict pandas to move to the mountians.” This is one of the reasons pandas are endangered.

Ever since the panda population has dwindled, The Chinese government sent about 600 pandas to different countries, where they could be held safe in a zoo without their habitat shrinking. Above is a map to where they live.

⚠️Endangered Species ☣️

Have you ever wondered why there are pandas in zoos? It’s because they are the property of China and have a role in a breeding program. They are in a breeding program because these cute, cuddly creatures are in danger, unfortunately.

Pandas are on the Endangered species list and their level is “vulnerable”, and one way to help pandas is to breed them and put them in good zoos where they are taken care of. There is a breeding program all over the world To increase the panda population. To do this, The Chinese government lends about 500 pandas to other countries. The government lends them to safe zoos where they don't have any predators threatening their panda cubs, and their food conveniently close to them.

This program has saved a percentage of the panda population. There are only about 1,800-2,000 pandas left in the world and 500 pandas left in the wild.

They are endangered because of habitat loss. This is due to infrastructure development. These poor pandas' habitats are shrinking. Fortunately, the Chinese government has made several reserves to help the panda population increase.
